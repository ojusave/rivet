## Steps

<Steps>
<Step title="Prerequisites">

- [Render account](https://render.com)
- Your RivetKit app
  - If you don't have one, see the [Quickstart](/docs/actors/quickstart) page or our [Examples](https://github.com/rivet-dev/rivet/tree/main/examples)
- Access to the [Rivet Cloud](https://dashboard.rivet.dev/) or a [self-hosted Rivet Engine](/docs/self-hosting)

</Step>
<Step title="Deploy to Render">

1. Connect your GitHub account to Render
2. Select your repository containing your RivetKit app
3. Render will automatically detect and deploy your app

See [Render's deployment docs](https://docs.render.com/deploys) for more details.

</Step>
<Step title="Set Environment Variables">

After creating your project on the Rivet dashboard, select Render as your provider. You'll be provided `RIVET_ENDPOINT` and `RIVET_PUBLIC_ENDPOINT` environment variables to add to your Render service.

See [Render's environment variables docs](https://docs.render.com/configure-environment-variables) for more details.

</Step>
<Step title="Connect to Rivet">

1. In your Render project, go to **Settings**
2. Copy your service URL (e.g. `https://my-app.onrender.com`)
3. On the Rivet dashboard, paste your URL with the `/api/rivet` path into the connect form (e.g. `https://my-app.onrender.com/api/rivet`)
4. Click "Done"

</Step>
<Step title="Configure Auto-Scaling (Optional)">

- [Configure Auto-Scaling](https://docs.render.com/scaling) to handle traffic spikes
- Consider upgrading from the free tier to avoid cold starts

</Step>

</Steps>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="502 Bad Gateway errors">

**Cause:** Your Render service may have spun down due to inactivity (free/starter tier).

**Solution:** Consider upgrading to a paid plan for always-on instances.

</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Build fails with 'vite: not found'">

**Cause:** Build dependencies are in `devDependencies` but Render runs `npm install --production` by default.

**Solution:** Move build tools (`vite`, `typescript`, etc.) to `dependencies` in your `package.json`, or set the environment variable:

```
NPM_CONFIG_PRODUCTION=false
```

</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
