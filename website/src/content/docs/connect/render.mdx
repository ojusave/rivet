## Steps

<Steps>
<Step title="Prerequisites">

- [Render account](https://render.com)
- Your RivetKit app
  - If you don't have one, see the [Quickstart](/docs/actors/quickstart) page or our [Examples](https://github.com/rivet-dev/rivet/tree/main/examples)
- Access to the [Rivet Cloud](https://dashboard.rivet.dev/) or a [self-hosted Rivet Engine](/docs/general/self-hosting)

</Step>
<Step title="Deploy to Render">

1. Go to the [Render Dashboard](https://dashboard.render.com/) and click **New > Web Service**
2. Connect your GitHub/GitLab repository containing your RivetKit app
3. Render will automatically detect and deploy your app

See [Render's deployment docs](https://render.com/docs/web-services) for more details.

</Step>
<Step title="Set Environment Variables">

After creating your project on the Rivet dashboard, select Render as your provider. You'll be provided `RIVET_ENDPOINT` and `RIVET_PUBLIC_ENDPOINT` environment variables to add to your Render service.

See [Render's environment variables docs](https://render.com/docs/configure-environment-variables) for more details.

</Step>
<Step title="Connect to Rivet">

1. In your Render service, note your service URL (e.g., `https://my-rivet-app.onrender.com`)
2. On the Rivet dashboard, paste your URL with the `/api/rivet` path into the connect form (e.g., `https://my-rivet-app.onrender.com/api/rivet`)
3. Click "Done"

</Step>
<Step title="Configure Auto-Scaling (Optional)">

- [Configure Auto-Scaling](https://render.com/docs/scaling) to handle traffic spikes
- Consider using a paid instance type for production workloads to avoid [free tier limitations](https://render.com/docs/free#free-web-services)

</Step>
</Steps>
